  <h1>Sardine, White Bean & Fennel Lemon Salad</h1>
  <div class="recipe-meta"><span>Prep: 15 min</span><span>Serves: 2</span><span>Heart-healthy</span></div>

  <p>Tinned sardines are one of the cheapest ways to get real omega-3s into dinner. White beans do the fibre work, fennel keeps it bright, and lemon stops the whole thing tasting worthy.</p>

  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>1 fennel bulb, very thinly sliced</li>
    <li>1 x 400g tin cannellini beans, drained and rinsed</li>
    <li>2 tins sardines in olive oil, drained lightly</li>
    <li>1 small shallot, finely sliced</li>
    <li>1 lemon, zest and juice</li>
    <li>2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil</li>
    <li>1 tsp Dijon mustard</li>
    <li>Small handful parsley or dill, chopped</li>
    <li>Salt and black pepper</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Method</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Put the fennel in a bowl with half the lemon juice and a pinch of salt. Toss and leave it for 5 minutes to soften slightly.</li>
    <li>Whisk the remaining lemon juice with the zest, olive oil, mustard, and black pepper.</li>
    <li>Add the beans, shallot, and herbs to the fennel. Toss with the dressing.</li>
    <li>Gently fold through the sardines in large pieces so they keep some shape.</li>
    <li>Taste and adjust with more lemon, salt, or pepper. Eat as is or with a slice of sourdough.</li>
  </ol>
