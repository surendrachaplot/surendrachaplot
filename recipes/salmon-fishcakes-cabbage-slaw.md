<article class="page recipe-detail">
  <h1>Salmon Fishcakes with Yogurt, Herbs & Cabbage Slaw</h1>
  <div class="recipe-meta"><span>Cook: 30 min</span><span>Serves: 2</span><span>Heart-healthy</span></div>

  <p>Fishcakes can go badly wrong if they turn into a breadcrumb delivery system. These stay mostly salmon, potato, herbs, and a sharp slaw on the side.</p>

  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>250g salmon fillet</li>
    <li>250g baby potatoes</li>
    <li>1 spring onion, finely sliced</li>
    <li>Small handful parsley or dill, chopped</li>
    <li>1 tsp Dijon mustard</li>
    <li>2 tsp olive oil</li>
    <li>1/4 small cabbage, finely shredded</li>
    <li>1 small carrot, grated</li>
    <li>2 tbsp Greek yogurt</li>
    <li>1 tbsp lemon juice</li>
    <li>Salt and black pepper</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Method</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Boil the potatoes for 12 to 15 minutes until tender. Drain and mash roughly.</li>
    <li>While they cook, roast or pan-cook the salmon until just done, then flake into a bowl.</li>
    <li>Mix the salmon with the mashed potato, spring onion, herbs, mustard, salt, and pepper. Form into 4 fishcakes.</li>
    <li>Pan-fry the fishcakes in the olive oil for 3 to 4 minutes on each side until golden.</li>
    <li>Toss the cabbage and carrot with the yogurt and lemon juice. Serve alongside the fishcakes.</li>
  </ol>
