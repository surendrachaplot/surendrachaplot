Goan-Style Fish Curry with Brown Rice

30 min · Serves 2

A lighter weeknight version of a Goan-style curry. Keep the coconut milk modest, let the tomatoes and spices do more of the work, and use brown rice if you want a steadier landing.

Ingredients

  • 150g brown basmati rice
  • 300g firm white fish, such as cod or haddock, cut into chunks
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 150ml light coconut milk
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste or juice of 1/2 lime
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh coriander, to serve

Method

  1. Cook the brown rice according to the pack instructions.
  2. Warm the olive oil in a pan. Cook the onion for 4 to 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the garlic, ginger, curry powder, and turmeric. Cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the tomatoes and coconut milk. Simmer for 10 minutes until slightly thickened.
  5. Stir in the tamarind, then add the fish chunks. Simmer gently for 5 to 6 minutes until just cooked through.
  6. Finish with coriander and serve with the brown rice.
