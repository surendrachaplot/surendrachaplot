Trout with Warm Lentils, Dill, and Mustard Vinaigrette

25 min · Serves 2

Trout has the same oily-fish credibility as salmon without feeling like the default setting. Lentils, dill, mustard. Clean, sharp, useful.

Ingredients

  • 2 trout fillets
  • 1 x 250g pouch cooked green lentils
  • 1 small shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • Small handful dill, chopped
  • 2 big handfuls rocket or spinach
  • Salt and black pepper

Method

  1. Season the trout and pan-fry skin-side down in a non-stick skillet for 4 to 5 minutes. Flip and cook for 1 to 2 minutes more.
  2. Warm the lentils in a saucepan or microwave.
  3. Whisk the shallot, mustard, vinegar, olive oil, dill, salt, and pepper into a vinaigrette.
  4. Toss the warm lentils with the greens and most of the dressing.
  5. Divide between plates, top with the trout, and spoon over the remaining dressing.
