  <h1>Salmon Soba Bowl with Edamame & Sesame Greens</h1>
  <div class="recipe-meta"><span>Cook: 25 min</span><span>Serves: 2</span><span>Heart-healthy</span></div>

  <p>This is the dependable salmon bowl: oily fish, greens, edamame, buckwheat noodles, and enough sesame to keep it interesting without wrecking the brief.</p>

  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>2 salmon fillets</li>
    <li>120g soba noodles</li>
    <li>150g shelled edamame</li>
    <li>2 large handfuls spinach or bok choy</li>
    <li>1 tbsp low-salt soy sauce</li>
    <li>1 tbsp white miso</li>
    <li>1 tsp honey</li>
    <li>1 tsp grated ginger</li>
    <li>1 tsp sesame oil</li>
    <li>1 tsp sesame seeds</li>
    <li>1 lime, cut into wedges</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Method</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Mix the soy sauce, miso, honey, and ginger. Rub over the salmon.</li>
    <li>Roast or air-fry the salmon at 220C for 10 to 12 minutes until just cooked.</li>
    <li>Cook the soba noodles. In the last 2 minutes, add the edamame. Drain and toss with the sesame oil.</li>
    <li>Wilt the greens in the hot noodle pan or quickly in a skillet with a splash of water.</li>
    <li>Divide the noodles, edamame, and greens between bowls. Top with salmon, sesame seeds, and lime wedges.</li>
  </ol>
