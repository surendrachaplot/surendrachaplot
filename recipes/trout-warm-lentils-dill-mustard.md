<article class="page recipe-detail">
  <h1>Trout with Warm Lentils, Dill & Mustard Vinaigrette</h1>
  <div class="recipe-meta"><span>Cook: 25 min</span><span>Serves: 2</span><span>Heart-healthy</span></div>

  <p>Trout has the same oily-fish credibility as salmon without feeling like the default setting. Lentils, dill, mustard. Clean, sharp, useful.</p>

  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>2 trout fillets</li>
    <li>1 x 250g pouch cooked green lentils</li>
    <li>1 small shallot, finely chopped</li>
    <li>1 tbsp Dijon mustard</li>
    <li>1 tbsp red wine vinegar</li>
    <li>2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil</li>
    <li>Small handful dill, chopped</li>
    <li>2 big handfuls rocket or spinach</li>
    <li>Salt and black pepper</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Method</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Season the trout and pan-fry skin-side down in a non-stick skillet for 4 to 5 minutes. Flip and cook for 1 to 2 minutes more.</li>
    <li>While the trout cooks, warm the lentils in a saucepan or microwave.</li>
    <li>Whisk the shallot, mustard, vinegar, olive oil, dill, salt, and pepper into a vinaigrette.</li>
    <li>Toss the warm lentils with the greens and most of the dressing.</li>
    <li>Divide between plates, top with the trout, and spoon over the remaining dressing.</li>
  </ol>
