  <h1>Goan-Style Fish Curry with Brown Rice</h1>
  <div class="recipe-meta"><span>Cook: 30 min</span><span>Serves: 2</span><span>Heart-healthy</span></div>

  <p>A lighter weeknight version of a Goan-style curry. Keep the coconut milk modest, let the tomatoes and spices do more of the work, and use brown rice if you want a steadier landing.</p>

  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>150g brown basmati rice</li>
    <li>300g firm white fish, such as cod or haddock, cut into chunks</li>
    <li>1 small onion, finely chopped</li>
    <li>2 garlic cloves, grated</li>
    <li>1 tsp grated ginger</li>
    <li>1 tbsp curry powder</li>
    <li>1/2 tsp ground turmeric</li>
    <li>1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes</li>
    <li>150ml light coconut milk</li>
    <li>1 tsp tamarind paste or juice of 1/2 lime</li>
    <li>1 tbsp olive oil</li>
    <li>Fresh coriander, to serve</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Method</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Cook the brown rice according to the pack instructions.</li>
    <li>Warm the olive oil in a pan. Cook the onion for 4 to 5 minutes until softened.</li>
    <li>Add the garlic, ginger, curry powder, and turmeric. Cook for 1 minute.</li>
    <li>Add the tomatoes and coconut milk. Simmer for 10 minutes until slightly thickened.</li>
    <li>Stir in the tamarind, then add the fish chunks. Simmer gently for 5 to 6 minutes until just cooked through.</li>
    <li>Finish with coriander and serve with the brown rice.</li>
  </ol>
