  <h1>Tuna, Cannellini Bean & Caper Pasta</h1>
  <div class="recipe-meta"><span>Cook: 20 min</span><span>Serves: 2</span><span>Heart-healthy</span></div>

  <p>The kind of pantry dinner that feels suspiciously competent. Use wholewheat pasta if you want to push fibre a bit harder.</p>

  <h2>Ingredients</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>160g wholewheat spaghetti or linguine</li>
    <li>1 x 400g tin cannellini beans, drained and rinsed</li>
    <li>1 tin tuna in olive oil, drained lightly</li>
    <li>1 tbsp capers</li>
    <li>2 garlic cloves, finely sliced</li>
    <li>1/2 tsp chilli flakes</li>
    <li>2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil</li>
    <li>1 lemon, zest and juice</li>
    <li>Small handful parsley, chopped</li>
    <li>Black pepper</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Method</h2>
  <ol>
    <li>Cook the pasta in salted water until al dente, reserving a mug of pasta water.</li>
    <li>Meanwhile, warm the olive oil in a large pan. Add the garlic, chilli flakes, and capers for 1 minute.</li>
    <li>Add the beans and a splash of pasta water. Stir for 2 minutes until warmed through.</li>
    <li>Add the tuna, lemon zest, and most of the lemon juice. Break the tuna into chunks.</li>
    <li>Toss in the drained pasta with more pasta water as needed to loosen. Finish with parsley, black pepper, and the remaining lemon juice.</li>
  </ol>
