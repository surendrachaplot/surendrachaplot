Trout with New Potatoes, Watercress, and Mustard Dressing

30 min · Serves 2

Same oily-fish logic, different shape. Potatoes keep it solid, watercress keeps it awake, mustard keeps it from drifting into blandness.

Ingredients

  • 2 trout fillets
  • 300g new potatoes, halved
  • 1 large handful watercress
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp wholegrain mustard
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp chopped dill or parsley

Method

  1. Boil the potatoes for 12 to 15 minutes until tender, then drain.
  2. Pan-fry or roast the trout for 8 to 10 minutes until just cooked.
  3. Whisk together the olive oil, Dijon, wholegrain mustard, lemon juice, and herbs.
  4. Toss the warm potatoes with most of the dressing and fold through the watercress so it softens slightly.
  5. Serve the trout over the potatoes and spoon over the rest of the dressing.
