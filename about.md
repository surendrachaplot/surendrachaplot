I'm from Udaipur — a city of lakes in Rajasthan. Pretty place. Home.

At 19, I co-founded a film production company. Made a Bollywood movie called "The Bet." The film was rubbish. Those days making it though were amazing. That's where I learned you don't need permission to build things. You just start.

Then came 21Fools. Started as a magazine, became an online greeting cards company. Now they make thoughtful, wonderful, sustainable products. All credit there to [this guy](https://www.instagram.com/divyanshuasopa). I'm just glad I was there at the start.

For the last decade or so, I've been learning the world of internet and money.

I was Head of Global Product at [Wise](https://wise.com/). We move money across borders without the usual fees and nonsense. We went from 6 million customers to 15.5 million, holdings from £3.7 billion to £21 billion, revenue from £420 million to £1.3 billion. (FY2021 to FY2025, for anyone keeping track.)

Those numbers sound big, but honestly? They just mean more people can send money home without getting ripped off. That's the bit that matters.

We did some firsts along the way: instant cross-border payments in Hong Kong, first central bank connections in Singapore and Australia, first multi-currency cards in APAC. We launched Wise Business invoicing — 625,000+ businesses now move £120 billion a year through it. We also closed a global Visa deal worth about £1 billion.

I've lived in Mumbai, Delhi, Jakarta, Singapore, and now I'm moving to London. Each city taught me something different.

These days, I'm also an angel investor. I back founders building things that matter — companies like [Cosmos](https://cosmos.video) and [Thinking Machines Lab](https://thinkingmachines.ai). Putting 20% of my income and time into the UK startup ecosystem.

I've spoken at the Hong Kong Fintech Festival (main stage) and done 25+ podcasts and publications. But honestly, I'd rather talk to a small group of founders in the trenches.

When I'm not working, I take photos ([@chaplot](https://instagram.com/chaplot)), read ([goodreads.com/chaplot](https://goodreads.com/chaplot)), or travel somewhere new. I'm a fan of Anthony Bourdain — liked that he was honest, curious, gave a damn about craft.

That's me. Kid from Udaipur who kept saying "why not" and building things. Still building.
