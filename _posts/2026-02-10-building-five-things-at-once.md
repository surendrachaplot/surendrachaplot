In April I'm moving to London. After seventeen years bouncing between Mumbai, Delhi, Jakarta, and Singapore, I'm planting somewhere. And I've decided to go in with both feet — not take a job, not consult quietly, but build.

Five things, simultaneously. This post is an honest account of what they are, why I'm doing them, and how I think about juggling all of it.

---

## The Five

**1. The Club** — A community-owned creative space in London. Weekday workshops, Saturday nightclub, co-working. Community-owned from day one, not as an afterthought. The model: people buy in to the ownership, not just the membership. I don't want to build a business that extracts from a neighbourhood. I want to build one that belongs to it.

Why this? Because every interesting creative space I've loved in London has eventually died when rent went up or the founder burned out. I think ownership is the solution. Community-owned pubs have survived. Community-owned music venues have survived. Why not this?

**2. Kinara** — Boutique travel. Groups of six to eight people, experiencing a place through the people who make it what it is. Local chefs, not restaurant critics. Ceramicists, not museums. Winemakers in their vineyards, not wine bars. Bourdain-style travel: real connection, real curiosity, no tourism polish.

First trip is Georgia in October. The Rtveli grape harvest. A winemaker's family, a long table in the vines, and wine made the same way for 8,000 years. That's the pitch.

**3. AI Consultancy** — Helping small businesses actually use AI. Not slide decks about the future. Not theoretical frameworks. Real implementations: automations, tools, workflows that save time or make money. Target is £1M ARR. The unlock is credibility — I spent eight years building product at Wise and watched AI go from curiosity to infrastructure.

**4. Privé** — Privacy-focused video calling. No accounts. No tracking. End-to-end encrypted. A link you share, a call you have, nothing stored. The thesis: privacy shouldn't be a premium feature. It should be the default.

**5. This website** — A build-in-public log. Not a polished newsletter. Not a personal brand exercise. Just an honest account of what I'm doing, what's working, what isn't, and what I'm learning.

---

## Why Five?

People ask this a lot, and I understand why it sounds crazy.

Here's my honest answer: I don't think these compete with each other for my time in the way most people assume. The AI Consultancy and Privé are technical builds — they need focused sprints. The Club and Kinara are relationship businesses — they need patience and networking, which I can do in parallel. The website is just writing, which I've always done anyway.

The deeper answer: I spent ten years optimising one thing at a time. Head of Product at Wise, focused completely, went from £420M to £1.3B revenue. That focus produced good results. It also meant I never tried the other things I cared about.

I'm not going to have another window like this. I've saved enough, I have skills that matter, I'm moving to a new city with the energy of a place just figuring out its next act. Now is the time.

---

## The Constraint

I'm doing all of this with one AI chief of staff, no team yet, and a move happening in six weeks.

The constraint is real. So the rules I've set myself:

1. One concrete output per project per week minimum
2. No planning documents that don't turn into real things
3. Public accountability — this site — so I can't pretend momentum I don't have
4. Ask for help early, not late

---

## What I'm Not Sure About

The Club needs a location. I haven't found one. Lewisham looks promising but the licensing question is still open.

Kinara needs customers. The itinerary is beautiful. The first person who signs up will tell me if the pitch is right.

Privé needs to be built. The landing page exists. The product doesn't. That's the next sprint.
